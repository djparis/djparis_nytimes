Dear Watchers,
Once upon a time, “what am I gonna watch tonight?” was a simple question to answer. No longer. There are now more TV shows on more networks and platforms with every passing second, and more movies available in more ways than ever before. But there’s only so much time in a day, and no one can watch everything.
Except us. Welcome to Watching, The New York Times’s what-to-watch guide. We comb through releases big and small, famous and esoteric to bring you our best recommendations for what you should be watching tonight, what you should be adding to your streaming watchlists, and what gems — new and old — you might have missed. Think of Watching as your way to build a balanced TV and movie diet; no one survives on prestige dramas (or network comedies) alone. Later this year we’ll be a website, but in the interim, you will find us in your inbox twice a week, and over at our Facebook page. Here’s a look at the week ahead.
— Margaret Lyons
 
Chuck Hodes/Fox
‘Empire’ Returns
After almost four months away, Fox’s hit drama returns Wednesday minus some buzz but back in maximum “Empire” form: schemes, secrets, sobbing, the presence of a firearm, a song. For a show that acts like it has a ton of stories happening at once, though, “Empire” circles back (and back and back) to the crisis from its first episode: Who is going take over the company? Even if you’ve fallen behind on Season 2, you can pick right up with Wednesday’s “Death Will Have His Day”; since characters on this show rarely act alone, they’re constantly explaining their motivations and tricks to one another. Watching the midseason finale helps, but isn’t essential.
Wednesday, Fox, 9 p.m. — Watch all of Season 1 and the first half of Season 2 at Hulu.
 
Worth Jumping in for Just One Episode
The first eight episodes of “The People v. O.J. Simpson” have brought a humanity, depth and context to the seemingly familiar story. But the show’s penultimate episode, “Manna From Heaven,” which could also be dubbed “The Mark Fuhrman Hate Speech Episode,” is particularly riveting (though, frankly, upsetting) and able to stand on its own.
Tuesday, FX, 10 p.m.; Watch the series on FX Now or purchase it on iTunes

Scott Everett White/The CW
Tune In: ‘Crazy Ex-Girlfriend’
The CW’s musical dark comedy has presented as solid and original a first season as any show in recent memory — funny and clever, unflinching and ambitious. The slightly deranged Rebecca (creator and star Rachel Bloom) finds herself in increasingly fraught high jinks thanks to her impulsive decision to move to California to be close to her summer-camp boyfriend, whom she’s never quite gotten over. Rebecca sings a majority of the musical numbers, so whenever another character has a full-on song, it’s a sign that they’re increasingly part of Rebecca’s world and outlook. Pay special attention to which supporting characters have big numbers in the season’s final three episodes.
Monday, The CW, 8 p.m.; Watch the five most recent episodes on Hulu or purchase the series at iTunes

Catch up on this British series
‘Call the Midwife’

This warm, emotional period drama returns Sunday on PBS for its fifth season. Cramming in 34 episodes by then is a lot; instead, just watch Season 4, which is available on Netflix. The show’s ostensible main character leaves at the end of Season 3, but creatively that winds up being a real boon for the series, which follows a group of nuns and midwives in post-World War II London.
 
Also This Week
“Lopez” George Lopez’s new quasi-autobiographical TV Land comedy, which premieres Wednesday, is a little flimsy from episode to episode, but it actually benefits by watching in two- to three-episode bursts.
“The Path” Hulu’s new drama, starring Aaron Paul, Hugh Dancy, and Michelle Monaghan, is set within a cult-like religious movement, and includes Jason Katims of “Friday Night Lights” among its executive producers. But it’s neither that gripping “oh-God-what-was-my-life-like-before-this” kind of show, nor even that engrossing, sob-inducing family drama (“Parenthood”) that Katims is known for. Hulu’s releasing the first two episodes Wednesday and then one a week after that.
“My Cat From Hell” The Animal Planet staple returns Saturday for a seventh season, consistently combining the morbid voyeurism of watching people in severe pet-induced distress with the blissful satisfaction of knowing cat wizard Jackson Galaxy will solve absolutely everything. It’s like if “Intervention” had only happy endings.
